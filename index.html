About ASHES Ghana

Who We Are

ASHES (African Students for Health, Environment, and Science) Ghana is a pioneering synthetic biology team from Ghana participating in the 2024 iGEM competition. Our multidisciplinary team brings together students from various fields including biology, engineering, social sciences, and communications.

We are committed to developing innovative solutions to address agricultural challenges in Ghana, with a focus on improving crop yield, reducing post-harvest losses, and enhancing food security for local communities.

Our team is guided by the principles of responsible innovation, sustainability, and community engagement, ensuring that our solutions are not only scientifically sound but also culturally appropriate and accessible to those who need them most.

Our Project

Project Overview

Our project focuses on developing a bio-based solution to combat post-harvest losses in cassava, a staple crop in Ghana. Through synthetic biology, we are engineering microorganisms that can extend shelf life and preserve nutritional value.

Methodology

We're utilizing cutting-edge genetic engineering techniques to design and construct genetic circuits in bacteria that produce natural preservatives and antimicrobial compounds specifically tailored for cassava preservation.

Expected Impact

Our solution aims to reduce post-harvest losses by up to 40%, increase farmer income, improve food security, and create new opportunities for value-added products in local agricultural communities across Ghana.
